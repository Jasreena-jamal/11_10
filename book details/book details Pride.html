Pride and Prejudice

by Jane Austen

★★★★☆ 4.29

Pride and Prejudice by Jane Austen is a timeless romance and social satire set in early 19th-century England. The story centers on Elizabeth Bennet, an intelligent and witty young woman from a respectable but modest family. Elizabeth’s life takes a turn when she meets the wealthy and reserved Mr. Darcy, whose aloof manner and apparent pride initially repel her.

As Elizabeth navigates societal expectations, family pressures, and her own judgments, she finds herself repeatedly crossing paths with Darcy. Through misunderstandings, surprising revelations, and moments of self-reflection, she discovers that first impressions aren’t always accurate. Darcy, too, learns to see beyond his assumptions, and they both begin to change in unexpected ways.

Austen’s novel explores themes of class, marriage, and morality with humor and insight, capturing the nuances of human relationships. Pride and Prejudice is a beloved classic, celebrated for its memorable characters, sharp social commentary, and exploration of the journey to understanding and love.

Release Date: January 28, 1813 Length: 279 pages
